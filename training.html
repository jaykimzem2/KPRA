FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is eligible for KPRA training?

KPRA training is open to all KPRA members. Some advanced programmes require a minimum level of membership (e.g., Professional or above). Non-members may attend select public workshops, but full programme access requires membership.

How do I earn the CPR designation?

Complete all 6 core modules of the KPRA CPR training programme, pass all 6 assessments with a minimum score of 70% each, and submit your application for the CPR designation. KPRA will review and issue the certificate within 4 weeks.

Are programmes available online?

Yes. KPRA offers both online (via the KPRA School Portal) and in-person training options. The online portal gives 24/7 access to all modules, readings and assessments. In-person workshops are held at the KPRA offices and other venues across Kenya.

What is the cost of KPRA training?

Training costs vary by programme. Members receive significant discounts on all KPRA training programmes. The full CPR certification course fees will be communicated upon registration. Contact us at info@kprakenya.co.ke for the current training schedule and fee structure.

Is the CPR designation recognized nationally?

Yes. The KPRA Certified Professional Realtor (CPR) designation is recognised across Kenya as a mark of excellence and professionalism. It distinguishes you in competitive bids, client relationships and regulatory environments.
